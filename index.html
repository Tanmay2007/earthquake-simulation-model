Working Principle

How the simulation works

Power Input

A DC motor with an offset weight is powered via the L298N motor driver, controlled by Arduino Uno sending PWM signals.

Vibration Generation

The rotating offset weight creates mechanical vibrations on the platform, simulating real seismic ground motion.

Energy Transfer

Two building models sit on the platform. The normal structure receives full vibration energy, causing visible sway and structural stress.

Base Isolation

The isolated building sits on rubber/sponge pads that absorb and dampen the vibration energy. It experiences significantly less movement.

Components

Hardware used in this model

Arduino Uno

Microcontroller brain. Sends PWM signals to control motor speed and manages the LCD display output.

L298N Motor Driver

Receives PWM from Arduino and drives the DC motor at variable speeds safely.

DC Motor

Core vibration source. A small nut/bolt acts as offset weight on the shaft to generate oscillations.

16x2 LCD Display

Shows real-time status of the motor. Connected via I2C module for minimal wiring.

Rubber / Sponge Pads

Act as base isolators under the isolated structure. Absorb and dampen seismic energy.

Cardboard + Sticks

Structural material for both building models. Lightweight and easy to observe vibration effects.
